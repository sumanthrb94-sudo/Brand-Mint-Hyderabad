We audited the homepages of a dozen Hyderabad builders (not naming names) and the same five mistakes kept showing up:
1. The fold sells features. The fold should sell a position.
2. The "About" page is a founder photo and a paragraph. It should be a worldview.
3. Project pages list amenities. They should narrate a building.
4. The phone number is the only call to action. There should be three, with intent levels (read more, save the brochure, book a visit).
5. The site loads in 8 seconds on 4G. Buyers don't wait.
None of these are design problems. They're brand problems being dressed as design.
The carousel walks through each one with a sketch of the wrong way, then a sketch of the right way — neutral, no shaming. If you run marketing at a builder and any of these are in your last quarterly review, treat this as a starter list, not a finished one.
