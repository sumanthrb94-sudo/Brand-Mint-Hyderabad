The Financial District has, for practical purposes, run out of large land parcels at the price point that made it attractive five years ago.
Three things follow.
First: the next wave of premium absorption moves to Narsingi and Kokapet's western fringe. It's already happening; the launches just haven't caught up to the absorption signal.
Second: per-sqft positioning in the Financial District itself splits into two tiers — a "view + brand" band that holds firm, and a "just an address" band that starts negotiating.
Third: the office demand that anchored the district is now leaking into Gachibowli, Manikonda, and the Tellapur road. Builders who reposition residential to track that leak will eat the next cycle.
We walk through each of these in the carousel, with the locality maps in the v13 system and a clean editorial read on each.
