In most cities, the tech district is master-planned.
Hi-Tech City wasn't. It was a few buildings on the edge of Madhapur in the late 1990s, anchored by Cyber Towers, and everything around it — the campuses, the malls, the apartment towers — accreted in response.
That's why the road network feels improvised. That's why three "Hi-Tech City" addresses can be 14 minutes apart on a Tuesday and 47 minutes apart on a Friday.
For real-estate reading, that history matters:
— "Hi-Tech City" as a locality label has stretched far beyond its original radius
— newer projects use the label more loosely than older ones
— the actual employment density now sits one ring further west, in Gachibowli and the Financial District
A reel-length history doesn't make you a planner. But it makes you a sharper buyer.
