- **Pillar:** Brand positioning for developers
- **Format:** Carousel (7 slides)
- **Hook:** Most builder Instagrams look like brochures from 2014.
- **Caption:**
A builder's Instagram is usually one of three things:
1. A flyer dump (renders, prices, "site visit now")
2. A diary (founder at events, ribbon cuttings, awards)
3. A silence (last post: 18 weeks ago)
None of these are brands. They're posters.
A brand on Instagram does three jobs at once:
— it tells you what the builder believes about the city
— it shows the same belief across formats (reel, carousel, hoarding, brochure)
— it stays consistent enough that a stranger can recognise the next post before they read it
That last one is the test. If you can hide the logo and a follower still knows it's you — it's a brand. If not, it's a feed.
In the carousel we break down seven small fixes any developer can make this quarter, with zero budget, zero new agency. Starting with the easiest: pick a position, not a tone.
