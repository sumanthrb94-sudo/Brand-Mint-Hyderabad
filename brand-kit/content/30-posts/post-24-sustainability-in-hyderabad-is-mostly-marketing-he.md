- **Hook:** Sustainability in Hyderabad is mostly marketing. Here's what isn't.
- **Caption:**
"Sustainable," in builder marketing, is doing the same work "luxury" did ten years ago: a lot of decoration, very little discrimination.
A solar panel on the clubhouse is not a sustainable project.
A "Lush green landscape" rendering is not a sustainable project.
A LEED-pending claim is not a finished sentence.
What we actually look at when we read a project for sustainability:
1. Site planning. Does it preserve existing trees, or did the renders show a forest the bulldozers removed?
2. Materials. Is concrete being supplemented or just used at scale?
3. Water. Rainwater harvesting and STP capacity, sized to actual occupancy.
4. Power. Solar load as a real percentage of common-area consumption.
5. Maintenance design. Sustainability that needs perfect operation isn't sustainability.
None of this is glamorous. All of it is verifiable.
Save this checklist. Carry it to your next site visit.
