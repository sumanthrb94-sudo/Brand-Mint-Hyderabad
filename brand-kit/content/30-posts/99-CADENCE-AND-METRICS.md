## Posting Cadence & Metrics-to-Watch

Use these as the studio's weekly review when looking at what's working across the 30 posts and what to iterate on:

- **Save rate, not like rate.** With a ~6-second average view-time signal, saves are the single best proxy for "the editorial landed." Track save rate by post — flag anything below 1.5% of reach as a hook problem (the visual isn't earning the dwell), and anything above 4% as a template worth re-running.
- **Hook retention curve, not average view-time.** Pull Instagram's audience retention chart on each Reel and look specifically at the 0–3 second drop. If a Reel loses more than 35% of viewers in the first 3 seconds, the hook line itself is the problem, not the content underneath. Re-shoot the open card, keep the rest.
- **Follower delta per pillar, weekly.** Tag each post with its pillar in a tracking sheet and watch which pillar contributes the most new follows over the 30 days. Our prior is that "Builder spotlights" and "Brand positioning for developers" will drive the highest follow conversion. If "Industry intel" outperforms, lean into more frequent data carousels.
- **Comment quality over comment count.** A post that gets 12 specific comments from named builders is worth more than one that gets 240 generic "Nice!" replies. Build a simple weekly tally of "named-account comments from people who'd pay us" — that's the real B2B signal and it points at which post types convert into DMs.
- **DM keyword tracking.** Every post that asks for a "DM '[keyword]'" should be tracked individually. If "audit," "calendar," and "system" all out-perform "tracker" and "index," reweight future CTAs toward brand-positioning DMs (which convert to B2B leads) over data DMs (which mostly convert to passive followers).
- **Reel-to-carousel reach ratio.** If Reels are pulling 4x the reach of carousels (likely), don't conclude carousels aren't working — conclude carousels are doing a different job (saves, long dwell, B2B credibility). Score them on their respective jobs, not on each other.
- **Posting-time discipline.** Hold the suggested slots for at least three full weeks before A/B-ing them. Switching times every other day prevents the algorithm from learning the rhythm. The slot plan above is opinionated; treat it as a hypothesis to validate, not a Monday-morning debate.
- **The Season Two readout.** At the end of the 30 days, rank all 30 posts by a composite score: 50% save rate + 30% follower delta + 20% qualified DM count. The top five posts become templates for Season Two. The bottom five get a post-mortem — not a "we'll do better" but a written note on what specifically the hook, format, or visual concept got wrong, so the next thirty posts don't repeat the miss.
