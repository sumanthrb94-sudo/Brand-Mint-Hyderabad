- **Caption:**
When a builder looks at a Hyderabad skyline, they're not seeing buildings. They're reading a stratigraphy — layers of time, money, and policy stacked on top of each other.
In the carousel, we teach you to read one skyline (the Kokapet–Financial District spine) the way an operator would.
Six layers:
1. The early-2000s mid-rises. Cement-grey, simple massing. Built for a city that hadn't been priced in yet.
2. The late-2000s tower experiments. Glass cladding, ornamental crowns. A first attempt at "premium."
3. The 2014–2018 vertical wave. Brand-name builders, IT-driven absorption, the first real branding attempts on the facade.
4. The 2019–2022 ultra-premium push. Designer collaborations, residential as architectural statement.
5. The 2023–2025 reset. Quieter design language, more discipline, fewer marketing fireworks.
6. The 2026 launches. Reading them is harder — too new to position, but the segment cues are already visible on the wraparounds.
Once you can read the layers, you can read the next one being built right now.
