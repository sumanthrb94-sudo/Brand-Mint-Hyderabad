### Post 28

- **#:** 28
- **Pillar:** Brand positioning for developers
- **Format:** Carousel (7 slides)
- **Hook:** A builder's content calendar, redesigned.
- **Caption:**
Most builder content calendars look like festival lists. Diwali graphic. New Year graphic. Independence Day graphic. International Women's Day graphic. Project milestone. Repeat.
We're proposing a different structure. Four content lanes, one rhythm, zero festival graphics.
The four lanes:
1. The Why — one post a month on what the builder believes about the city
2. The What — one post a week on a specific project, told as a portrait, not a pitch
3. The Where — one post a fortnight on the locality (history, current read, future)
4. The Who — one post a month on the team, the architect, the on-site engineer, the long-tenured operations head — anyone but the founder
Stop posting festival graphics. Start posting positions.
The carousel includes a one-month sample calendar built on this framework. Save it as a starter. If you're a builder reading this and want yours redesigned — DM "calendar."
Follow @brandmint.studios.
- **Visual concept:** Carousel slide 1 — title in serif, with a designed "calendar grid" beneath rendered in v13 styling (no real platform logos, just a 4-week grid). Slides 2–5 each take one content lane with a serif lane name, a one-paragraph definition in mono, and a sample post topic. Slide 6 — the four lanes assembled into one month. Slide 7 — a clean "no festival graphics" sign-off in serif.
