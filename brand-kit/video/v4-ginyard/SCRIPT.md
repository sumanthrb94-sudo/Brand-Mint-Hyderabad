# Brand Mint — "Built to Ship" — 12s Reel script (v4 · Ginyard-grammar)

*Reference template: <https://canva.link/402uvoziftavy5g> — "Purple, White
and Black Modern Animated Business Advertising Mobile Video" (1080×1920,
1 page). We borrow its **grammar** (broken-line question hook · alternating
solid colour blocks · rapid services stack · contact close) and apply
Brand Mint's tokens. We deliberately drop revenue claims and lead with
hook patterns proven to convert.*

---

## Why no revenue numbers

Revenue claims work for warm audiences who already trust the studio.
They do not perform on cold paid Reels for two reasons:

1. **Disbelief tax.** Cold viewers discount any unverified claim. The
   ad's CTR pays the tax — "+₹42.6 Cr" reads as marketing, not proof.
2. **Wrong audience.** A revenue number qualifies leads who think
   in their own revenue, but the buyer here is the *founder*. Founders
   convert on a different signal: "do you understand my problem?"

So this cut leads with a **problem-shaped question hook**, then
positions, then closes with **scarcity + specificity**.

## Hook taxonomy — what converts on Reels (no revenue)

| Pattern              | Mechanism                                  | Example used in v4 |
|----------------------|--------------------------------------------|--------------------|
| Question hook        | Forces the viewer to answer mentally → opt-in micro-commitment in 1 frame | "*Want to ship something that lasts?*" |
| Negation / antagonist| Cuts contrast with a competitor category   | "Not an agency. A studio." |
| Specificity hook     | Concrete place / time / number             | "Built in HITEC City. Booking Q3 2026." |
| Scarcity hook        | Time-bound or slot-bound CTA               | "Booking Q3 2026 — 3 founders." (in CTA scene) |
| Rapid services stack | Three nouns in three beats (Sites · Tools · AI) — burst of perceived range | beats 7–9 |

The four are stacked deliberately: **question → negation → services →
scarcity**. By the CTA, the viewer has self-identified
(founder shipping a product), discounted the competitor (agency), seen
the range (sites + tools + AI), and felt the floor close (Q3 2026,
limited slots).

## Beat-by-beat copy

### 1 · "Want to" (0.5s, cream Paper bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600, 180pt, Ink #0A0E0C
- Top-left anchored at x=80, y=400 — leaves negative space below
- Subtle scale-up 1.0 → 1.02 over the half-second

### 2 · "ship" (0.4s, mint Mint3 bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600, 280pt, Cream #F5F1EA
- Center anchored — this is the *verb* that the question pivots on

### 3 · "something" (0.5s, black bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600, 200pt, Cream
- Bottom-anchored at y=1400 — implies "more is coming"

### 4 · "that lasts?" (1.4s, cream bg, longest beat in act 1)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600, **all italic mint #10B981**, 150pt
- Centered. This is the **payoff line** of the question hook — italic
  signature is carried by the entire phrase (cairosvg's tspan-with-
  centered-anchor breaks mixed inline styling, so beats 4/5/6/9 carry
  the italic-emphasis convention at the beat level rather than within
  the line)

### 5 · "We're a studio." (1.6s, mint bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600, 110pt, Cream
- Centered. **First identity reveal** — the answer to the question
- Italic emphasis is reserved for beat 6 (the antagonist) to maximise
  the upright→italic contrast across the cut

### 6 · "Not an agency." (1.2s, black bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600 **italic**, 110pt, Cream
- Centered. **The antagonist** — defines us by what we are *not*. The
  whole line carries the italic so the negation reads as a soft retort

### 7 · "Sites." (0.7s, cream bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 700, 280pt, Ink
- Center. Single noun, big. The first of three quick beats

### 8 · "Tools." (0.7s, black bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 700, 280pt, Mint #10B981
- Center

### 9 · "AI." (0.8s, cream bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 600 **italic**, 360pt, Mint #10B981
- Centered. "AI." gets the colour + italic combo to land it as the
  emphasis term of the services stack — and to weigh the same as the
  longer five-letter words in beats 7 & 8

### 10 · "Built in HITEC City." (1.2s, black bg)
- Plus Jakarta Sans 500, 88pt, Cream — display, not titanic
- Centered. Specificity hook — Hyderabad-grounded, anti-generic

### 11 · "Booking Q3 2026." (1.2s, mint bg)
- "Booking" in Plus Jakarta 500 Ink 80pt
- "Q3 2026." in JetBrains Mono 500 Ink 100pt with 0.06em tracking
- Centered, stacked. Scarcity hook — concrete date forces decision

### 12 · "Brand Mint. / Start a project →" (2.0s, black with mint glow)
- Monogram (gradient circle + single-stroke M) fades in scaling 0.85 →
  1.0 with overshoot
- "Brand Mint." in Plus Jakarta 600, 86pt Cream below
- CTA pill: Mint #10B981 bg, Cream label "Start a project →", 720×140

### 13 · "brandmint.studio" (1.0s, same bg)
- URL types in character-by-character in JetBrains Mono 38pt Mint
- Blinking cursor at the end for 200ms
- Composition fades out together over the final 400ms

## Sound design

Same Reels-policy-safe original score, retuned for the higher BPM:

- Drone bed: 55 Hz + 82.4 Hz + 110 Hz sine + brown-noise air (same as v3)
- **Whoosh on every whip cut** (beats 2, 3, 4, 7, 8, 9, 11) — 0.18s
  bandpassed noise sweep
- **Soft kick** under the *that lasts?* beat (beat 4 entry) — 60 Hz
  sine 0.15s
- **Pluck** on the services stack (beats 7, 8, 9) — perfect-fifth ping
- **Cymbal swell** under beat 12 (the CTA) — 1.5s rising pink noise
- Final low note fades out across beat 13

Total: ~13 SFX events on a 12s cut — keeps the cut feeling kinetic
without ever crossing into chaotic.

## Why this script will out-convert v3 on cold Reels

| Lever                          | v3 (Compound)         | v4 (Built to Ship)     |
|--------------------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|
| First-frame opt-in mechanism   | Stat — impressive but passive | Question — forces a mental answer |
| Belief required                | Believe the ₹42.6 Cr  | Zero — just self-identify  |
| Beats in first 3 seconds       | 1 stat + setup        | 6 beats (4-word question + studio reveal start) — higher density |
| Antagonist                     | Implicit (proof gap)  | Explicit ("Not an agency.") |
| Specificity                    | Number-led            | Place + date — harder to fake |
| Scarcity                       | Soft ("Booking Q3")   | Same line gets a full beat at 1.2s |

(senior services for founders in India). Will validate as soon as you
