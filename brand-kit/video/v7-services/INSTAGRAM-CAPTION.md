## Why the no-prices hook works on Instagram specifically

Instagram truncates feed-view captions after ~125 characters. The first 116 of the no-prices caption are **the full six-service list** plus the *"one senior team"* anchor:

> `Six things we ship: custom websites, internal tools, brand systems, AI integrations, paid ads, SEO. One senior team.`

That fits *just* under the truncation point, so in the grid-scroll view a founder sees the **breadth of capability** before the "more" cut-off — not a generic positioning line. Without prices, specificity now comes from the stack names later in the caption (Next.js, Claude, Meta), not from numbers.

**When to use which:**
- **No-prices version** for top-of-funnel awareness — the post needs to lead with what you do, not what it costs. Higher hook rate, broader reach.
- **Priced version** for bottom-of-funnel conversion — when you want self-selected leads who can afford the launch pricing. Lower volume, higher LQS.

## A/B variants (no-prices · cycle every 14 days)

Hold the body identical. Swap only the opening hook.

| Cell | Opening hook | Mechanism |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| A *(control)* | `Six things we ship: custom websites, internal tools, brand systems, AI integrations, paid ads, SEO. One senior team.` | Breadth-first — broad pull |
| B *(antagonist)* | `Most agencies leave you with a logo. We leave you with a working platform. Six services, end to end.` | Anti-positioning |
| C *(geography)* | `Built in HITEC City. Shipped worldwide. Six services for founders who outgrew the template.` | Geo-led — high local resonance |
| D *(speed)* | `From discovery call to live in 7 weeks. Six services. One senior team. We don't sell templates.` | Speed-led — for founders mid-renewal |

**Winner metric:** DMs / 1,000 impressions (not raw visits). Volume rewards loud hooks; DM-conversion rewards the right ones.

## Predicted performance signals

| Metric | Target on a cold IG audience | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| Hook rate (3-sec views / impressions) | ≥ 28% | Whether the service list stops the scroll |
| 15-sec hold rate | ≥ 9% | Whether the service stack carries through |
| Profile visits / 1k impressions | ≥ 7 | Whether the brand mark stuck |
| Saves / 1k impressions | ≥ 12 | Saves are the IG "vote" — the algorithm ranks saves above likes |
| DMs / 1k impressions | ≥ 0.5 | CTA conversion |

If hook rate < 22% after 1,000 impressions, swap to **B** (the antagonist variant).
If DMs / 1k < 0.3, swap to the **priced** version — it qualifies leads harder.

## When to switch from no-prices to priced

- After the no-prices post has run 14 days and hook rate is **above** 28%: switch to priced for the next cycle. Higher hook rate means people are watching long enough that pricing won't scare them off — and the priced version qualifies leads harder.
- When launch pricing ends (after the first 10 founders this quarter): retire the priced version permanently, use no-prices as the default.
