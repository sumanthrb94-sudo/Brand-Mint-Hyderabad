# Brand Mint — "Studio." — 50s anthem · Apple grammar

*"How does Apple do advertisement?" — applied. The opposite of v4: no
rapid cuts, no service stacks, no scarcity, no CTA pill. Eight slow
beats, ~6 seconds each, on near-pure-black. Mint appears at exactly
**two** moments. The brand mark is the hero, not the headline.*

Reference vocabulary: Apple's *"Hello."* (iPhone X teaser), *"Designed
by Apple in California."* (film + book), *"Shot on iPhone"* still-ad
series, *"Crush!"* (iPad Pro 2024).

---

## The eight rules of this cut

1. **One object per frame.** Single sentence, single colour, no lists.
2. **Black is the surface.** 90% of frames are `#000000` with cream
   `#F5F1EA` type. Mint enters twice.
3. **Silence carries the cut.** Drone bed only. No whooshes, no plucks.
   One musical swell at the reveal beat. Then it goes quiet again.
4. **Slow.** Average beat is 6.0s — six times slower than v4.
5. **Crossfades only.** No whip-pans, no slideleft, no fadeblack.
6. **No cinema bars.** Apple doesn't letterbox; we follow.
7. **No CTA pill, no URL banner.** The wordmark + URL stamp is the
   close — restraint, not conversion.
8. **The italic is the only typographic emphasis.** Used once.

## What's missing on purpose

Everything v4 has that this cut refuses:

| v4 has | v5 says no | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Question hook | — | Apple doesn't ask. Apple states. |
| Antagonist (`Not an agency.`) | — | Stated indirectly through `A studio.` + `In a city of agencies.` |
| Services stack | — | "Apple does not list features in 6-second cuts." |
| Specificity stat | — | Place is enough. No numbers. |
| Scarcity (`Booking Q3 2026`) | — | Apple sells abundance, not scarcity |
| CTA pill | — | Wordmark closes the cut. |
| Whoosh/pluck/kick hits | — | Restraint. Single musical swell. |

What's left is just the brand and the place. That's the entire point.

## How this differs from v3 and v4 (one-line each)

- **v3 "Compound"** — proof-led Stripe-x-Mubi minimalist. *Believe the stat.*
- **v4 "Built to Ship"** — Ginyard-grammar punch reel. *Choose us, fast.*
- **v5 "Studio."** — Apple-grammar anthem. *We exist. That is enough.*

Use cases:
- **v3** — paid Reels, warm audiences, conversion campaigns
- **v4** — paid Reels, cold audiences, low-bid tests
- **v5** — site hero loop, LinkedIn pinned video, YouTube long-form,
  film opener at events, internal North-star reference for the studio's voice

## Why Apple's grammar fits Brand Mint

The brand-strategy doc (`brand-mint-admin/02-BRAND-STRATEGY.md`) names
the three-word identity: **Senior. Specific. Indian.** Apple's grammar
is *Confident. Specific. American.* — swap California for Hyderabad
and the structures line up: one sentence, one place, one mark, full
stops.

The vocabulary blacklist (no *innovative*, *seamless*, *leverage*,
*synergy*…) is the same blacklist Apple has been operating on since
1997. We are not borrowing — we are recognising the same craft.
