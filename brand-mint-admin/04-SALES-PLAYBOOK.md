# 04 · Sales Playbook

## The funnel

```
Inbound          ┐
LinkedIn DM      ├──►  Discovery call (30 min)  ──►  Mint workshop  ──►  Proposal  ──►  Signed SOW
Founder warm     │     [qualification]                [paid OR free      [fixed-scope    [50% advance,
intro            │                                     based on tier]     within 48 hrs]   work begins]
Partner referral ┘
```

Target rates:
- Inbound → discovery: **40%**
- Discovery → workshop: **50%**
- Workshop → proposal: **80%** (workshops are pre-qualified)
- Proposal → signed: **60%**

End-to-end: roughly **10-12% of inbound becomes paid work.** A 18 leads/month pipeline → 2 signed engagements/month.

## Source mix (target by month 6)

| Source | Target % |
|---|---|
| Site inbound | 35% |
| Founder warm intro | 25% |
| LinkedIn outbound | 15% |
| Partner / referral | 15% |
| Speaking / writing | 10% |

If any single channel exceeds 50%, the pipeline is fragile. Diversify deliberately.

## Discovery call (30 min)

Use the script in `sales/discovery-call-script.md`. Goal of the call:

1. Confirm they fit ICP (use rubric in `sales/qualification-rubric.md`)
2. Surface the metric they want moved
3. Decide which Mint workshop tier (paid or free)
4. Set proposal date

If they're not a fit, **kill the deal politely on the call**. Don't drag.

## Mint workshop (the qualification gate)

Two tiers:

### Free workshop (45 min, async-prep)

For Tier 1 / Tier 2 ICP. Goal: write the SOW in collaboration with the buyer so the proposal is a formality.

Deliverable: 1-page brief with their name on it.

### Paid workshop — `₹25K` (90 min, in-person or video)

For Tier 3 ICP, larger engagements (₹8 L+), or anyone you're not sure about. The fee is credited 100% against the engagement if they sign within 14 days. If they don't sign, you keep the ₹25K and they keep the artefact.

This is the single most powerful sales tool we have. Use it.

## Proposal — within 48 hours of workshop

Use the template in `contracts/proposal-template.md`. Every proposal includes:

1. **One paragraph restating their goal** (in their language, with their numbers)
2. **Scope** — what's in, what's not (be ruthless about not)
3. **Deliverables** — bulleted, dated
4. **Timeline** — Gantt-style with key dates
5. **Investment** — fixed price, GST extra, payment schedule
6. **Team** — who's leading (founder name + 1 collaborator)
7. **Acceptance criteria** — how we'll know it's done
8. **Next step** — one sentence with a link to e-sign or accept

Page count: 4 max. Anything longer signals insecurity.

## Pricing posture

You will be asked "can you do it cheaper" by 80% of buyers. Three responses:

1. **No, but we can reduce scope** — point to a smaller tier and remove deliverables
2. **No** — when the price is already correct
3. **Yes, but** — only if you're getting something back (faster decision, longer commitment, case-study rights)

Never discount just to win. The cheapest project is always the most expensive to deliver.

## Negotiation: the three levers

When a deal stalls on price, pull *one* of these — never two:

1. **Reduce scope** — drop a page, drop a feature, smaller team
2. **Shift schedule** — start later (we slot it into a slow month)
3. **Add commitment** — convert to retainer, get logo rights, get a case study, get an intro

## The case-study trade

For any project under ₹3 L, ask for case study rights *as part of the price*. "We'll do this at our Studio tier in exchange for permission to publish results 90 days post-launch." 60% of founders say yes; the case study is worth ₹2-5 L in pipeline.

## Loss reasons — track every one

Every lost deal goes in a quarterly review. Categories:

- Price (we were too expensive)
- Timing (they weren't ready)
- Scope mismatch (they wanted something we don't do)
- Trust (they went with a more established brand)
- Internal politics (decision pushed back)
- No reply (ghost)

If "Price" exceeds 30% of losses → we're priced wrong.
If "Trust" exceeds 25% → we need more case studies / press / referrals.
If "No reply" exceeds 40% → our discovery → workshop transition is broken.

## Proposal-to-signed automation

Use HelloSign or DocuSeal (free, self-hostable) for e-signature. Every proposal that's signed triggers:

1. Auto-generated GST invoice for 50% advance
2. Slack/Loom welcome message from the lead
3. Calendar invite for kickoff
4. `ops/kickoff-checklist.md` opened for the engagement

This 4-step automation should fire within 30 minutes of signature. If it takes longer, the buyer's adrenaline drains and the project starts cold.

## Renewal / upsell motion

For any retainer client at month 2:

- **Demo a one-off project** alongside the retainer that would compound their results (e.g. "I noticed your sign-up flow — want a 2-week sprint to fix it for ₹2 L?")
- **Anchor against quarterly review** so the upsell conversation isn't a surprise

For any project client at delivery:

- **Lead with retainer offer** in the handover meeting: "You've got the site; the next 90 days is when content, performance, and tools start compounding. Here's what a ₹1.25 L/mo retainer would look like."

Target: **30% of project clients convert to retainer within 60 days.**
