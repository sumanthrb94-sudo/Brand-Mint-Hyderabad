# Weekly Cadence — How a Studio Week Runs

The rhythm. Same shape every week, regardless of how many engagements are active.

## Monday

### 9:30 — Internal standup (15 min, Slack huddle)

For each active engagement:
- Where we are vs. plan
- What's blocking
- What's shipping this week

### 10:00 — Founder's metrics review (30 min, alone)

Update the dashboard from `12-METRICS-AND-KPIS.md`:
- Cash, pipeline, MRR, AR, burn
- Top 3 risks for the week

### 10:30 — Sales / pipeline review (30 min)

Walk through every lead in stages 1-5. Move stages, log losses, schedule next steps.

### 11:00–13:00 — Sales work block

Write proposals, follow up on outstanding workshops, reach out to 5 cold/warm leads.

### 14:00–18:00 — Deep work

Deepest work day of the week. No client calls except absolute emergencies.

## Tuesday

### Morning — Client engagement work

Block calendar 9:30-13:00 for active engagement work. Daily Looms recorded by 18:00 IST.

### Afternoon — Client calls / demos

Schedule client meetings here. Stakeholder reviews, mid-week check-ins.

## Wednesday

### Morning — Content + writing

The CEO's writing day. Long-form post or 2 LinkedIn posts. Newsletter draft mid-month.

### Afternoon — Engagement work

## Thursday

### Morning — Engagement work

### Afternoon — Discovery calls

Schedule new-lead discovery calls this afternoon by default. 30-min slots, 4 max in a day.

## Friday

### 09:00–10:00 — Demo prep

Polish the week's deliverables for the 4 PM demos.

### 10:00–14:00 — Engagement work

### 14:00–17:00 — Demos

Most engagements demo at 4 PM IST Friday. Stack them back-to-back: 1500 / 1600 / 1700. 45 min each, 15-min buffer.

### 17:00–17:30 — Studio retro

Internal 30 min:
- What worked this week
- What didn't
- What we're changing next week
- Wins to celebrate (named)

### 17:30 — End of week

Send weekly summary email to active retainer clients (template below). Sign off.

## Templates

### Daily Loom (engagement, weeks 1-2)

```
[Project name] · Day [N]

Today:
- [Specific output 1]
- [Specific output 2]
- [Specific output 3]

Tomorrow:
- [Plan]

Blocking / I need from you:
- [If anything]
```

5 minutes. Send by 18:00 IST. Use Loom + post link in Slack.

### Weekly retainer recap email (Friday EOD)

Subject: `[Brand Mint] Week of [date] — [Client] retainer`

```
Hi [name],

This week:
- [Specific shipped item with link]
- [Specific shipped item]
- [Specific shipped item]

Numbers since last week:
- [Metric 1]: [delta]
- [Metric 2]: [delta]

Next week we'll:
- [Item 1]
- [Item 2]

Heads up:
- [Anything we need from them, or any risk]

— [Name], Brand Mint
```

### Weekly Friday demo agenda (60 min)

| Minutes | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0-5 | Recap last week's decisions |
| 5-30 | Walk through this week's shipped work |
| 30-45 | Open questions / decisions needed |
| 45-55 | Next week's plan |
| 55-60 | Anything outside the project |

End every demo with:
> "What's the one thing that, if we'd nailed it this week, would have made the demo even better?"

This question costs 30 seconds and reveals more than 10 NPS surveys.

## Saturday & Sunday

**Default: do not work.**

Exceptions: client emergency that costs them money if not addressed; founder's personal writing time (treated as recreation, not work).

The studio runs hard Mon-Fri. The boundary is the discipline.

## What "off-cadence" looks like

Signs the rhythm has slipped:

- Weekly demos getting cancelled or pushed
- Daily Looms skipped without communicating
- Friday retro turning into "let's just push through"
- Monday metrics review skipped 2 weeks in a row
- Saturday/Sunday work happening regularly

Each is a leading indicator. If 2 happen in the same week, the founder calls a 30-min reset on Friday.

## What good cadence looks like

- Demos happen on time, every time
- Clients can predict your rhythm without asking
- The studio's calendar has white space (deep work blocks visible)
- Friday retro is short because most weeks went well
- Monday is calm because Friday wrapped clean
