# Kickoff Checklist — Day 0 of Every Engagement

To be completed within 24 hours of SOW signature. Owner: lead on the project (founder for now). Run sequentially — don't batch.

## Before kickoff call

- [ ] SOW countersigned and stored in `03-proposal/` of the engagement folder
- [ ] First invoice issued (50% advance) and confirmed paid before any work
- [ ] Engagement folder created in Drive with the 8 standard subfolders (see `05-OPERATIONS-MANUAL.md`)
- [ ] Slack Connect channel created: `#client-[name]-bm`
- [ ] Calendar invite for kickoff: 60 min, with agenda
- [ ] Notion / Linear project created with milestones from the SOW timeline
- [ ] Internal team aligned (Slack DM with the team: scope, dates, hand-off points)

## Kickoff call agenda (60 minutes)

| Minutes | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0-5 | Re-introductions; Studio team intros |
| 5-15 | Re-state goals (the "north star sentence") and read-back of the SOW deliverables |
| 15-30 | Working model: who's on Slack, who attends weekly demos, how decisions get made |
| 30-45 | Working session — review the brief, surface assumptions, list open questions |
| 45-55 | Calendar — block weekly demo times for the next 6 weeks |
| 55-60 | First-week deliverables agreed; next contact |

## Access checklist (during or right after kickoff)

Studio gives client:

- [ ] Slack channel invite
- [ ] Notion project URL (read-only or guest)
- [ ] Loom workspace invite (for daily updates)
- [ ] Studio team contact list (lead, designer, engineer, ops)

Client gives Studio:

- [ ] Existing brand assets (current logo, fonts, photos)
- [ ] Brand voice references (any prior copy they like / don't)
- [ ] Domain access OR DNS contact
- [ ] Hosting credentials OR commitment to spin up new (Vercel + Cloudflare recommended)
- [ ] Analytics access (GA, Plausible, Meta Pixel)
- [ ] CRM access if integrating leads (HubSpot, Salesforce)
- [ ] Stakeholder list — names, roles, decision authority
- [ ] Calendar availability for weekly demos

Use a single shared Google Doc for the access checklist; tick items off as they land.

## Day-1 deliverable from Studio

Within 24 hours of kickoff, send the client a single Loom (10 min max) covering:

1. What we heard: paraphrase their goal in your words
2. What we're going to do this week: 3 specific outputs by day
3. What we need from them this week
4. Where to find everything (Notion link, Slack channel)

This Loom is the *single most underrated trust-builder* in the studio. Clients screenshot it and forward to their teams.

## Internal kickoff brief (template)

Every engagement gets a 1-page internal brief. Owner: lead. Read by everyone on the team before any work begins.

```
ENGAGEMENT: [Client name]
SOW REF:    BM-[YYYYMM]-[short]
DURATION:   [X weeks], starts [date], ends [date]
VALUE:      ₹[amount]

GOAL:
[One paragraph in their voice. The "north star sentence."]

ICP:
[Who their customers are, in one paragraph.]

WHAT WE'RE BUILDING:
[Bulleted list of deliverables. Match the SOW.]

WHAT WE'RE NOT BUILDING:
[Anything we know they might ask for but isn't in scope.]

CRITICAL CONSTRAINTS:
- [Any hard date — fundraise, launch, etc.]
- [Any tech constraint — must use X, can't use Y]
- [Any sensitivity — customer, market, political]

SUCCESS CRITERIA:
[Two or three concrete metrics or artefacts. Reference the SOW acceptance criteria.]

WHO'S ON IT:
- Lead: [name]
- Designer: [name]
- Engineer: [name]
- Producer: [name if hire 3 made]

CADENCE:
- Daily Loom (W0-W2)
- Weekly demo: [day, time IST]
- Slack: #client-[name]-bm

RED FLAGS WE'VE NOTED:
[Any pre-call concerns from qualification — be honest with the team.]
```

Commit this to the engagement folder under `01-discovery/`.

## First-week rituals

- **Daily Loom** to client (4-7 min, end of each working day)
- **Daily standup** internal in Slack (15 min, 9:30 AM IST)
- **Friday demo** — first one is "visual direction lock" for design engagements
- **Friday internal review** — 30 min team retro on the week

If the client doesn't watch the Looms by day 4, raise it directly:
> "Quick check — are the daily Looms useful? Happy to switch to written updates if video isn't your format."

Better to ask than assume. Sometimes the answer is "switch to weekly," and that's fine.
