# Discovery Call Script — 30 Minutes

The first call. Goal: qualify in or out, surface the metric they want moved, decide on workshop tier, set the proposal date.

## Pre-call (5 min before)

- Read their site
- Pull their LinkedIn
- Check if they raised recently or are about to (Crunchbase, Tracxn)
- Note one specific thing about their company to mention (proves you did homework)

## Opening — minute 0–2

> "Hey `[NAME]`, thanks for blocking the 30 min. I keep these tight — I'll spend the first 20 understanding what you're trying to do, and the last 10 telling you whether we're a fit. If we are, we'll talk next steps. If we're not, I'll point you to someone who is. Sound fair?"

Two things this opening does:
1. Sets a 30-min budget so they don't drift
2. Signals you'll say no — which makes your yes more credible

## Discovery — minute 2–22

Five questions, in order. Don't rearrange.

### Q1. "Walk me through your business in two minutes."

Listen for:
- Stage (pre-revenue, ₹X Cr ARR, etc.)
- Buyer (who pays them)
- Recent triggers (raise, launch, hire)

### Q2. "What's prompting this conversation right now? Why now and not 3 months ago?"

This is the most important question. Buyers who can answer have urgency. Buyers who can't are exploring.

### Q3. "If we're sitting on a call 6 months from now and this engagement was a clear win, what specifically changed?"

Force a metric. Push back if they say "more leads" — ask "from what to what?"

If they can't give you a number after one prompt, they're not ready. Note this for the rubric.

### Q4. "What have you tried before? What worked, what didn't?"

This surfaces:
- Bad agency experiences (you'll need to reassure)
- Unrealistic expectations from past wins
- The actual gap they're trying to fill

### Q5. "Who's making this decision, and what's the timeline?"

Listen for:
- Single decision-maker (good) vs committee (slower)
- Budget already allocated (good) vs being negotiated (slower)
- Decision in 2 weeks (good) vs end of year (cold)

## Pitch — minute 22–28

Compress to 5 minutes. Not a presentation — a conversation about fit.

> "Let me quickly tell you how we usually work, and you tell me where this lands."
>
> "We're a senior-only studio in HITEC City. Every engagement is led by an 8+ year operator — me, in your case. We do `[SERVICE THAT MATCHES THEIR NEED]` typically in `[TIMELINE]` for `[PRICE RANGE]`."
>
> "What makes us different from the agencies you might compare us to — three things. We charge in INR, ship in weeks not months, and the person you talk to is the person who builds. That last one's the biggest."

Drop one specific case study that pattern-matches their situation.

> "For example, we worked with `[CLIENT]` on `[SIMILAR PROBLEM]` and shipped in `[TIMELINE]`, which moved their `[METRIC]` by `[NUMBER]`."

Don't oversell. Stop talking when you've made the point.

## Closing — minute 28–30

Three possible closes:

### Close A — Fast yes (Tier 1 + budget + timeline)

> "Sounds like a fit. Next step: I'd like to run a 90-min Mint workshop. It's free if you're ready to engage in 14 days; if you're shopping, it's ₹25K and credited to the project if you sign. Which makes sense for you?"

Send Notion form + calendar link by EOD.

### Close B — Slow yes (fit, but timing or budget unclear)

> "Sounds like the fit is right but timing's not yet. Want me to send a 1-page positioning artefact next week so we have something concrete when you're ready? No pressure either way."

This buys you mind-share without burning workshop time.

### Close C — Polite no (not a fit)

> "Honestly, this is probably not a Brand Mint engagement. Here's who I'd talk to instead: `[REFERRAL]`. Happy to make the intro."

Don't water down the no. Be useful, then move on.

## Things to listen for (red flags)

- "We're getting quotes from 5 agencies" → low-trust commodity buyer; expect race to bottom
- "Our budget is whatever it takes" → they have no number, will balk at first price
- "Can you start next week?" with no scope → they're panicking, not buying
- "I'll need to check with my partner / wife / co-founder before signing" → real decision-maker isn't on the call

## Things to listen for (green flags)

- "I read your `[SPECIFIC POST]`" → warm
- "We've already allocated ₹X" → has budget approved
- "We tried X agency and it didn't work because Y" → specific = thoughtful
- "Founder of `[CLIENT YOU WORKED WITH]` recommended you" → cold lead pipeline working

## Post-call (within 30 min)

1. Update sales tracker (stage, value, next step)
2. Send recap email — 5 bullets, what we agreed, calendar invite for next step
3. Score them in `qualification-rubric.md`
4. If no/maybe: log loss reason

## Always be willing to walk

If a call sounds wrong by minute 10, you can shorten:

> "I want to be respectful of your time — based on what you've shared, I don't think Brand Mint is the right fit. Here's why, and here's where I'd send you instead."

Walking away from a bad-fit deal preserves the bandwidth to win three good-fit deals. Founders remember a graceful no longer than they remember a forced yes.
