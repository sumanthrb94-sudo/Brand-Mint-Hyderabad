# Qualification Rubric — Score the Lead 0–100

After every discovery call, score the lead. Do it within 30 minutes while the conversation is fresh. The score determines next step.

## How to score

Five dimensions, 20 points each. Honest scoring beats optimistic scoring every time.

### 1. Fit (ICP match) — /20

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 20 | Bullseye Tier 1 ICP — Series A/B Indian SaaS or D2C ₹5+ Cr |
| 15 | Tier 2 — adjacent fit (e.g., Indian SaaS Series Seed, large D2C, GCC midmarket) |
| 10 | Tier 3 — outside our usual but interesting (e.g., enterprise rebrand) |
| 5 | Edge case — possible but the ICP needs to expand |
| 0 | Not a fit — wrong industry, wrong stage, anti-ICP |

### 2. Urgency — /20

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 20 | Decision in <30 days; budget approved; trigger event named |
| 15 | Decision in 30-60 days; budget being finalised |
| 10 | Decision in 60-90 days; budget exists but not committed |
| 5 | "When the time is right" — no timeline |
| 0 | Browsing, no specific need |

### 3. Budget — /20

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 20 | Stated budget meets or exceeds our floor for the service |
| 15 | Budget within 25% below floor; could close with scope adjustment |
| 10 | Budget unclear but they paid for similar work before |
| 5 | Budget seems below floor; price negotiation will be hard |
| 0 | "What does it cost?" with no anchor — high risk |

### 4. Decision authority — /20

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 20 | Founder/CEO on the call AND signs the cheque |
| 15 | Founder on call, partner needs to approve (1 round) |
| 10 | Senior leader on call, founder approval needed |
| 5 | Mid-level on call; will champion to founder |
| 0 | Junior researcher / "consultant tasked with finding agencies" |

### 5. Trust signals — /20

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 20 | Came in via warm intro from a respected source; mentioned specific Brand Mint work |
| 15 | Came in via partner referral or LinkedIn outreach to founder |
| 10 | Cold inbound from site; thoughtful initial message |
| 5 | Cold inbound; generic message; multiple agencies in parallel |
| 0 | Mass-RFP looking pattern; suspicious profile |

## Total score → action

| Score | Tier | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80-100 | A — Hot | Free Mint workshop within 7 days. Proposal in 48 hours. |
| 60-79 | B — Warm | Paid (₹25K) Mint workshop OR free with shorter SOW commitment. |
| 40-59 | C — Cool | Send 1-page positioning artefact. Re-touch in 30 days. |
| 20-39 | D — Cold | Polite no. Refer if possible. Drop into nurture (newsletter). |
| 0-19 | F — No fit | Polite no. Don't add to any list. |

## Examples

### Example 1: Score 92 (A — Hot)

- Fit: 20 — Series A SaaS, ₹40 Cr ARR, perfect ICP
- Urgency: 18 — closing Series B in 8 weeks; site must be ready
- Budget: 20 — quoted ₹4-6 L range; ours: ₹3.5-5 L
- Authority: 20 — CEO + founding designer on call
- Trust: 14 — cold inbound but referenced 2 of our LinkedIn posts

→ Workshop scheduled for Tuesday. Proposal sent Thursday.

### Example 2: Score 38 (D — Cold)

- Fit: 10 — agency in our space (would be a vendor partner, not customer)
- Urgency: 5 — "exploring options"
- Budget: 5 — wouldn't share
- Authority: 10 — founder on call but needs partner approval
- Trust: 8 — generic LinkedIn DM

→ Polite no. Sent Plus Jakarta resource link as good-faith gesture.

## What if you keep getting B and C scores?

Two diagnostics:

1. **Inbound is wrong audience** → tighten content positioning, narrow LinkedIn copy, refresh the site headline
2. **You're qualifying too leniently** → re-score last 10 leads against this rubric. If the average climbs to 60+ when you're stricter, your discovery is too generous

If you keep getting A scores but they don't close — the **proposal** is the problem, not qualification. Audit `04-SALES-PLAYBOOK.md` and `contracts/proposal-template.md`.
